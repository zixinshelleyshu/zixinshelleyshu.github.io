She is passionate about applying artificial intelligence to medicine to create tools that support clinical decision-making and improve patient outcomes. Currently, she is a third-year PhD student at the University of Bern, supervised by Prof. Mauricio Reyes. Her research focuses on developing reliable and interpretable AI systems for medical image analysis. She collaborates closely with clinicians to bridge the gap between technical innovation and clinical practice, integrating explainable AI and uncertainty estimation to ensure transparency, trust, and real-world impact in healthcare. She obtained her Bachelor's degree in Statistics, Economics, and Finance from University College London (UCL) and her Master's degree in Data Science from ETH Zurich.

## Research Interests

- Medical image analysis
- Reliable AI systems
- Interpretable machine learning
- Healthcare applications
